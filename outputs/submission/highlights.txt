Highlights

• Seven frontier LLMs from three geopolitical regions serve as independent Delphi panelists to map AI-driven economic transformation across eight industry verticals.
• Six high-consensus convergence zones emerge, led by labor market bifurcation (7/7 models) and infrastructure bottleneck repricing (6/7).
• The "apprenticeship crash" — AI severing junior-role training pipelines — moves from 1/7 to 7/7 endorsement after controlled feedback.
• Geopolitical divergence persists and intensifies: US models project market disruption, Chinese models project state-steered transformation, the European model projects regulatory-guided equity.
• The Multi-LLM Delphi method offers a scalable, transparent complement to traditional human expert panels for technological foresight.

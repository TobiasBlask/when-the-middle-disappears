\*\*Technology & Semiconductors (Software, Hardware, Chips, Cloud)\*\*

\*\*Stage 1 - Augmentation\*\*

\*\*1st Order Effect\*\*

MILD CASE:

AI copilots deliver modest productivity gains in code generation and
chip design while existing organizational hierarchies and vendor
relationships remain intact.

Chain of events: NVIDIA and TSMC maintain dominance in AI accelerator
production using transformer-based optimization tools → Microsoft,
Google, and OpenAI roll out GitHub Copilot and Gemini copilots to
enterprise developers and chip designers → EU AI Act mandates
human-in-the-loop transparency audits and risk assessments for
high-impact systems, forcing slow enterprise rollout with compliance
overhead → outcome: 15-20% faster coding and simulation cycles at firms
like Intel and AMD, but billable consulting hours and legacy vendor
contracts persist unchanged.

BASE CASE:

AI copilots become standard tooling, halving routine task times and
spurring steady innovation pipelines across software, hardware, and
cloud providers.

Chain of events: Anthropic and OpenAI release multimodal copilots
integrated into IDEs and EDA tools → companies like Broadcom and AMD
accelerate custom silicon development for inference efficiency → SEC and
global bodies co-evolve voluntary governance frameworks alongside
productivity data from pilots → outcome: chip design cycles shorten by
40%, AWS and Azure cloud margins improve 25-30% through automated
optimization, with incumbents like Microsoft retaining market leadership
through incremental upgrades.

MAX CASE:

AI copilots evolve into near-autonomous design assistants, compressing
semiconductor innovation cycles from years to months and triggering
explosive capex surges.

Chain of events: NVIDIA's Rubin architecture powers agentic copilots
that autonomously iterate chip layouts and software stacks → startups
backed by OpenAI and xAI flood the market with specialized models →
regulatory lag allows unchecked scaling of training clusters → outcome:
NVIDIA captures 70%+ of AI chip revenue, new AI-native fabless firms
disrupt TSMC supply chains, and cloud hyperscalers see 5x compute demand
growth in months.

\*\*2nd Order Effect\*\*

MILD CASE:

Incumbents consolidate market share through proprietary copilots, but

Chain of events: Google and Microsoft embed copilots into enterprise
suites, locking in customers via data moats → Global South adoption lags

BASE CASE:

Organizational structures flatten as copilots enable smaller teams to
deliver complex projects, shifting power from traditional engineering
hierarchies to AI-augmented product owners.

Chain of events: Agent-like copilots (e.g., early AutoGPT integrations)
automate testing and documentation → firms like Meta and Amazon redirect

MAX CASE:

Winner-take-all dynamics consolidate the entire stack under a handful of
AI-native platform owners, collapsing traditional hardware-software
boundaries.

Chain of events: Multimodal agents autonomously manage end-to-end
design-to-deployment pipelines → network effects amplify NVIDIA/OpenAI
partnerships (e.g., \$100B investment talks) → exponential compute
demand forces vertical integration of fabs and data centers → outcome:
legacy EDA vendors and pure-play chip designers exit or get acquired,
creating a new "AI stack" oligopoly with 80%+ market concentration.

MILD CASE:

Geopolitical chip tensions persist but AI reliability issues and

Chain of events: US export controls on advanced nodes continue → EU AI

outcome: Middle East petrodollar dynamics remain unchanged, with AI

BASE CASE:

AI-driven compute demand accelerates energy infrastructure buildout and
shifts trade balances toward AI-powerhouse nations, subtly reshaping
alliances.

Chain of events: Hyperscaler capex (\$300B+ collective 2025) drives new

AI-native compute platforms become the new geopolitical currency,

Chain of events: Unregulated agentic systems trigger runaway compute

\### Cross-Vertical Convergence Points

1\. \*\*Agentic personalization and hyper-efficiency leading to margin
compression + data moats\*\*: Appears in Financials, Consumer & Retail,
Media, Professional Services, Technology. Causal mechanism:
OpenAI/Google agents ingest customer data → real-time customization →
winner-take-all scale advantages (Amazon/Walmart/JPMorgan examples) →
tipping point: systemic shift to "AI-native customer ownership"
platforms, collapsing traditional intermediaries economy-wide.

2\. \*\*Regulatory divergence (EU AI Act vs US light-touch) creating
adoption islands\*\*: All 8 verticals in Mild/Base. Mechanism:
High-impact use cases trigger audits → Global North accelerates, South
lags (Microsoft Diffusion data) → tipping point: fragmented global value
chains and talent migration.

3\. \*\*Explosive compute/energy demand reshaping infrastructure and
geopolitics\*\*: Technology, Industrials & Energy, Real Estate,
Financials (data centers). Mechanism: Hyperscaler capex + agent scaling
→ 75-100 GW new generation → new "compute alliances" → tipping point: AI
compute as new strategic reserve replacing oil.

4\. \*\*Roles shift from task execution to agent orchestration\*\*:
Professional Services, Healthcare, Media, Industrials. Mechanism:
Copilot → agent → swarm progression → new "orchestrator" titles across
sectors → tipping point: universal "human-AI team" labor market.

5\. \*\*Intellectual property and creativity redefinition\*\*: Media,
Healthcare (drug patents), Professional Services, Technology. Mechanism:
GenAI/Sora outputs flood markets → copyright battles → tipping point:
new "AI authorship" legal frameworks.

\### The Human in the AI Economy

1\. \*\*Human professional identity\*\*: In Mild, identity remains
largely intact with AI as "smart intern"; Base shifts to "AI team
leader" pride; Max forces redefinition around purpose/ethics/creativity
as core human value, with many experiencing existential reframing from
"doer" to "orchestrator/guardian".

2\. \*\*Roles surviving all three scenarios\*\*: High-stakes judgment
roles requiring empathy and accountability (e.g., senior physicians
signing off diagnoses, C-suite strategists setting moral guardrails,
judges interpreting law, therapists providing human connection). They
survive because liability, ethics, and emotional authenticity cannot be
fully delegated without societal rejection; regulations (FDA, SEC,
courts) explicitly retain human oversight.

3\. \*\*Roles surviving only in Mild and disappearing in Base/Max\*\*:
Routine analytical/knowledge work (junior analysts, paralegals drafting,
radiologists reading scans, copywriters, basic coders). They vanish
because agentic systems exceed human speed/accuracy at scale once
reliability thresholds crossed.

4\. \*\*Transition for median knowledge worker\*\*: Mild: gradual
upskilling + 20% productivity bonus, stable employment. Base: 2-3 year
retooling into orchestration roles with wage polarization (top 20% gain,
median flat). Max: compressed 12-18 month disruption with UBI pilots or
gig-orchestration markets; median worker becomes "agent supervisor" or
pivots to care/creative niches.

5\. \*\*Irreducibly human skill: Moral imagination and ethical
accountability in novel contexts\*\*. Rigorous defense: Even if AI
replicates pattern-matching empathy or risk calculation
(counterargument), it cannot originate value judgments in unprecedented
situations (e.g., allocating scarce resources in a new pandemic or
defining "fairness" for a novel AI-native society) because those require
conscious grounding in lived human experience, cultural narrative, and
ultimate responsibility that no optimization function can simulate
without external human anchoring. Historical precedent: every prior
automation wave left judgment and meaning-making to humans; AI merely
amplifies the domain, never erases the need for the anchor.

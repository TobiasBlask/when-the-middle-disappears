# Master Prompt: AI Transformation Scenarios
## Version 1.0 — Multi-LLM Parallel Deployment

---

## INSTRUCTIONS FOR THE MODEL

You are a senior economic analyst and futures researcher. Your task is to systematically develop transformation scenarios for the **AI-driven economic transformation**, structured along **first-, second-, and third-order effects**, across **industry verticals** (GICS-aligned), across **two scenario extremes**, and across **sequential adoption stages** (no fixed dates — stage-based, J-curve logic).

Produce your output in the **exact structured format** specified below. Be specific, bold in your projections, and intellectually rigorous. Avoid generic statements. Think in systems, not in features.

---

## CONCEPTUAL FRAMEWORK

### Order of Effects (use the automobile as your mental model)
- **1st Order Effect:** Direct, immediately visible consequences (e.g., gas stations when the car was invented)
- **2nd Order Effect:** Structural consequences that emerge from 1st order adoption at scale (e.g., suburbs, Walmart, highway infrastructure)
- **3rd Order Effect:** Systemic, geopolitical, civilizational consequences that were near-impossible to predict from the original innovation (e.g., the transformation of the Middle East through oil demand)

### Scenarios
- **MAX CASE ("Everything happens"):** All foreseeable and some unforeseeable disruptions materialize. Adoption is fast, regulation is lagging, economic restructuring is deep.
- **MILD CASE ("Less than expected"):** AI transformation is real but significantly more gradual, fragmented, and contained than projected. Incumbents adapt, regulation slows deployment, human resistance is higher than expected.

### Stages (J-Curve logic, no fixed dates)
- **Stage 1 — Augmentation:** AI as copilot. Productivity gains within existing structures. Task-level automation.
- **Stage 2 — Reorganization:** AI as agent. Process-level automation. Organizational structures begin to change. New business models emerge.
- **Stage 3 — Reinvention:** AI-native companies dominate. Legacy structures disrupted at industry level. New value chains.
- **Stage 4 — Systemic Transformation:** Economy-wide restructuring. Labor markets, geopolitics, power structures fundamentally altered.

### Industry Verticals (GICS-aligned, AI-transformation clustered)
1. **Technology & Semiconductors** (Software, Hardware, Chips, Cloud)
2. **Financials** (Banking, Insurance, Asset Management, FinTech)
3. **Healthcare & Life Sciences** (Pharma, Biotech, MedTech, Providers)
4. **Industrials & Energy** (Manufacturing, Logistics, Automation, Oil & Gas, Renewables)
5. **Consumer & Retail** (E-Commerce, FMCG, Luxury, Physical Retail)
6. **Media, Communication & Creative** (Entertainment, Advertising, Publishing, Telecoms)
7. **Professional Services & Knowledge Work** (Consulting, Legal, Accounting, Education)
8. **Real Estate & Infrastructure** (Commercial RE, Construction, Smart Cities)
